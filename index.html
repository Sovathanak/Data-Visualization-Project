Global Population from 1950 to 2020

This visualization aims to shows data of global population as well as contributing factors/details that may/may not have any effect or relation to the population in each of the countries and continents.

Global Population Density

Bangladesh maintained top 1 in terms of population density throughout the 70 years. Bangladesh had around 316 people per km² 1950 which was already more twice as much as India's and China's population density combined.

By year 2000, population density in Bangladesh passed 1000 people per km² and peaked in 2020 where the population density rose to a whopping 1346 people per km².



Child Mortality vs Child Birth (per 10,000) from 1950 to 2019

Child mortality per 10,000 and Child birth per 10,000 seems to have greater numbers for countries in Africa and Asia than those in other regions.

In 1950, most countries in Africa such as Mali has a Child mortality per 10,000 of around 405 and Child birth per 10,000 of 526. It is much higher compared to countries with immense population such as India and China even though Nigeria's population, compared to these countries is rather small.

By 2019, the Child mortality per 10,000 around the globe has seen a significant decrease especially in Africa, namely for countries like Mali, where it dropped to only 94 compared to a whopping 405 in 1950. Despite the significant drop in Child mortality per 10,000 in Africa, the Child birth per 10,000 has stayed roughly the same amount compared to previous years.



Population Growth by Continents

Corresponding to the data shown above, where Africa and Asia has higher Child birth per 10,000, it is evident here where the population growth changes depending on Child birth. This is evident when we reach 2019 and possibly onwards where the population growth rate in Africa is way ahead of other other continents as well as the global average with 2.472 growth rate by 2019, over 1% more than the global average.
